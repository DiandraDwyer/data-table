Police Draw Weapons on Male Suspects Nearly Half the Time

By Diandra Dwyer November 5 at 2:00 PM

BLOOMINGTON, In. â€“ Data from The Bloomington Police Department show that male suspects apprehended between April 2017 and July 2017 had a weapon drawn on them 48% of the time.

Sixty-seven percent of these men were recorded as "normal," meaning they were not impaired at the time of the encounter.

Out of all male suspects who had a weapon drawn on them, 46% had a weapon as well. None of the officers in these encounters were injured. Twenty-three percent of all suspects who had a weapon drawn on them were injured.

Men were four times as likely to have a weapon drawn on them as women. The data show that Caucasian Non-Hispanic men were almost twice as likely to have a weapon drawn on them as African American Non-Hispanic men.

According to Bloomington's Census Data, the population of Bloomington is 83% Caucasian and 4.6% African American, which means the total proportion of Caucasian men is greater.

Weapons, fighting, warrants and suspicious activity were the top four most common charges for men who had weapons drawn on them.
